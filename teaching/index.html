Overview

I have been actively involved in teaching and student supervision at both Strathmore University (2015-2017) and the University of New South Wales (UNSW) (2020-2023), delivering lectures, supervising labs, and mentoring research students. My teaching spans mobile development (iOS/Apple Development), blockchain systems, privacy-preserving technologies, and cybersecurity, bridging theoretical concepts with practical applications in both mobile and decentralized systems.

Teaching Philosophy

My Teaching Approach

I emphasize practical application, hands-on learning, research integration, and mentorship to prepare students for real-world challenges.

Practical Application

Connecting theoretical concepts with real-world implementations in blockchain and cybersecurity, ensuring students can apply knowledge in industry settings.

Hands-on Learning

Providing students with opportunities to build and evaluate systems, fostering experiential learning through project-based assignments.

Research Integration

Encouraging students to explore cutting-edge research problems, bridging the gap between academic research and practical applications.

Mentorship

Supporting students in their academic and professional development, providing guidance beyond the classroom to help them achieve their career goals.

I believe in fostering critical thinking and problem-solving skills while maintaining a strong foundation in security principles and cryptographic techniques. The goal is to prepare students not just for exams, but for real-world challenges in cybersecurity and decentralized systems.
